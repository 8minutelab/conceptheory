About

Original objects shaped by warmth, memory, and imagined places.

Concept Theory is a design-led collection of miniature ambient objects that merge architectural detail, transit symbolism, poetic interfaces, and cinematic light into collectible desk-scale works.

What the collection explores

The work draws from magical realism, Japanese visual culture, quiet public infrastructure, domestic rituals, miniature architecture, village transport cues, and interior fragments that feel emotionally inhabited.

Rather than functioning as toys or conventional gadgets, these pieces are intended as refined decorative objects with atmosphere, narrative suggestion, and practical emotional value. Some frame time. Some suggest status or routine. Others simply hold a scene that allows the viewer to pause.

Across the collection, the emphasis remains consistent: restrained form, believable materiality, soft light, and the feeling that a small object can open a larger imaginative space.
